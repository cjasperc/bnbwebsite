Why Composite Works in Norwich

Timber looks good in year one. Composite still looks good in year fifteen.

Norfolk has 630mm of rainfall per year. Timber decking absorbs it. Over two or three winters it warps, greys, and develops the slippery algae that every homeowner dreads. Composite boards are capped, moisture can't get in. They look the same in February as they do in August.

Thinking About Paving?

Composite decking vs paving, the honest comparison.

For many Eaton gardens, paving seems like the obvious choice. Here's why the longer-term picture often points to composite decking.

Composite Decking Paving / Patio
Maintenance An occasional rinse. Nothing else needed, ever. Annual weeding, jet washing, and re-pointing. Moss returns within months in Norwich's wet climate.
Lifespan 25+ years with a full manufacturer warranty. 10–15 years before cracking, subsidence, or significant discolouration needs addressing.
Winter performance Slip-resistant texture maintained year-round, even when wet and cold. Paving with algae growth becomes dangerously slippery in winter months, a real liability in family gardens.
Design flexibility Multi-level, raised, wraparound, decking creates defined zones and changes how a garden feels. Flat surface only. Limited ability to add levels or create distinct outdoor rooms.
Long-term cost Higher upfront but near-zero running cost. Cheaper over a 15+ year horizon. Lower upfront but ongoing maintenance costs add up. Re-laying within 15 years is common.
Kerb appeal A well-designed deck adds visible value and improves how the house presents. Standard paving rarely differentiates a property. Worn paving can actively reduce appeal.
