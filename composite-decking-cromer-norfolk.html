Outdoor Living in Cromer

Cromer has one of the best outlooks on the Norfolk coast. Make the most of it.

Cromer and the surrounding area has a mix of permanent residents, holiday homeowners, and people who let their properties out throughout the season. What they share is that the outdoor space matters, whether that's a sea-view terrace, a sheltered garden deck, or a yard that needs to handle guests year after year without falling apart.

The coastal environment is hard on conventional materials. Cromer's exposed position means stronger winds, heavier salt air, and faster deterioration than you'd see inland. Timber greys and softens. Paving grows algae on the shaded north-facing aspects. We install composite decking that handles all of this without any help from you, built for exactly the conditions Cromer creates.

  • Exposed coastal conditions, no problem

    Salt air, strong winds, heavy rainfall, composite handles all of Cromer's climate without deteriorating. No rust, no rot, no grey weathering.

  • Ideal for holiday lets

    Premium composite decking raises a listing's appeal, earns better reviews, and justifies higher nightly rates, with no maintenance burden between bookings.

  • Zero maintenance required

    No annual treatment schedule. Rinse it once or twice a year and that's the full extent of it. In a coastal climate where conventional materials need constant attention, that's the difference.

  • TrexPRO Platinum, Norfolk's only installer

    The highest Trex certification available. Your installation is fully warranted and built to the standard the manufacturer expects.

Why Composite Works in Cromer

Cromer's exposed position tests outdoor materials harder than most of Norfolk. Composite is designed for it.

Thinking About Paving?

Composite decking vs paving in Cromer, the honest comparison.

In a coastal town like Cromer, the advantages of composite over conventional paving compound quickly. Here's the picture.

Composite Decking Paving / Patio
Coastal durability Unaffected by salt air, moisture, or UV. 25-year warranty even in exposed coastal conditions. Salt efflorescence, algae growth, and accelerated weathering are all common on paving in Cromer's exposed position.
Maintenance Rinse once or twice a year. No other work ever required. Jet washing, re-pointing, moss treatment, annually, and more often in the growing season in a coastal climate.
Holiday let appeal Premium-looking decking stands out in listing photos and guests mention it in reviews. Justifies higher nightly rates. Worn coastal paving detracts from listing appeal and is rarely noted positively in guest reviews.
Safety when wet Textured slip-resistant surface in all conditions. Safe year-round for guests and family. Algae-covered paving in Cromer's wet winters becomes genuinely hazardous, a liability risk for holiday let owners in particular.
Long-term cost Higher upfront, but near-zero running cost. Cheaper over 15 years than paving with ongoing maintenance. Lower upfront but regular maintenance costs add up. Likely re-laying within 12–15 years in coastal conditions.
