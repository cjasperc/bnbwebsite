Considering Paving Instead?

Composite decking vs paving, what Southwold homeowners need to know.

Paving is a reasonable choice in some contexts. For a coastal property that's occupied part-time, the maintenance demands and long-term performance tell a different story.

Composite Decking Paving / Patio
Salt air exposure Unaffected. Composite doesn't rust, corrode, or degrade with salt exposure. Salt causes efflorescence on paving. Pointing deteriorates. Metal edging rusts.
Between visits Needs nothing. Returns looking exactly as you left it regardless of season. Moss, weeds, and algae establish quickly in unoccupied periods, especially through a damp Suffolk winter.
Long-term cost Higher upfront. Zero running cost. Cheaper over 15+ years when maintenance costs are factored in. Lower initial cost. Ongoing maintenance, re-pointing, and eventual re-laying add significant expense.
Aesthetic longevity 25-year colour warranty. No weathering, no discolouration, no tired appearance. Paving visibly ages. Coastal properties show this quickly, within 3–4 years in most cases.
Holiday let premium High-quality decking with a coastal feel photographs exceptionally. Directly supports premium listing positioning. Tired paving is conspicuous in photos and actively undermines a premium positioning.
Safety year-round Textured, non-slip surface performs safely in wet and cold conditions. Algae-covered paving near the coast becomes dangerously slippery through autumn and winter.
