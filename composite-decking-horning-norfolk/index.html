Waterside Properties in Horning

The Broads is one of the most beautiful settings in England. Your outdoor space should match it.

Horning sits right on the River Bure, and many properties here have direct water frontage, mooring areas, or gardens that sit within metres of the riverbank. It's a stunning setting, and a demanding one for outdoor materials. High groundwater, constant moisture in the air, and the cycle of seasonal occupation mean conventional timber and paving solutions simply don't hold up.

We install composite decking throughout the northern Broads. The material is impervious to moisture, won't rot or swell at the water's edge, and needs nothing between visits. For a holiday property or second home, that's not a luxury, it's the practical choice.

  • Built for the water's edge

    Composite boards don't absorb moisture, swell, or develop the mould and rot that affect timber in permanently damp environments.

  • Ideal for seasonal properties

    A Horning holiday home might sit empty from October to March. Composite returns looking exactly as you left it, no winter deterioration.

  • Zero annual maintenance

    No treating, staining, or pressure washing. In a moisture-heavy environment, that's the difference between a deck that stays beautiful and one you're constantly chasing.

  • TrexPRO Platinum installed

    Norfolk's only TrexPRO Platinum installer. Warranty-backed installation from the only local team with the highest Trex certification.

Why Composite Wins on the Broads

Riverside conditions destroy conventional decking. Composite is designed for exactly this.

The Norfolk Broads has one of the highest water table levels in England. Timber installed near the river absorbs ground moisture even before rainfall is considered. Moss, algae, swollen boards, and rot follow within two or three seasons. Composite boards are fully capped, moisture is irrelevant. They perform the same whether your garden is ten metres from the river or right at the bank.

Thinking About Paving?

Composite decking vs paving near water, the honest comparison.

In waterside settings, the differences between decking and paving are more pronounced than anywhere else. Here's the honest picture.

Composite Decking Paving / Patio
Moisture resistance Fully capped, water cannot penetrate the board. No swelling, no rot, no mould growth. Paving absorbs moisture through joints. In high water table areas like the Broads, subsidence and cracking occur faster.
Winter appearance Returns looking exactly as you left it. No winter deterioration during unoccupied periods. Algae, moss, and weed growth in joints over winter, significant on return after months away.
Safety when wet Textured, slip-resistant surface throughout. Safe near the water's edge even in wet conditions. Algae-covered paving near waterways is genuinely dangerous, particularly for guests unfamiliar with the property.
Lifespan 25+ years with manufacturer warranty, in waterside conditions, timber lasts 3–5. 8–12 years in high-moisture environments before significant remedial work is needed.
Maintenance An occasional rinse. Nothing else required, ever. Jet washing, re-pointing, weed treatment, more frequent near water where growth accelerates.
